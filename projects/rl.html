Humanoid Manipulation & Locomotion Exploration

Hands-on exploration with the Unitree H1 to study coordinated manipulation and basic locomotion using reinforcement-learning policies, stability tuning and safety-first hardware trials. Work covered whole-body control behavior, joint-level tuning, and the practical challenges of bringing simulation results onto real hardware.

What is the project?

Evaluate the capabilities and limits of the H1 for combined movement and simple manipulation. I investigated stability, response latency, and upper-body coordination within 4-DOF arms, while examining how RL locomotion policies transfer from sim to real.

Key challenges

  • Sim-to-real gap: RL policies that walked well in simulation produced unstable or inconsistent gait on hardware.
  • Unmodelled dynamics & sensor noise: differences in environment and sensors vs. sim.
  • Hardware safety & reliability: early trials risked violent vibrations; auxiliary components like dexterous hands needed frequent fixes.
  • Tuning complexity: selecting gains (Kp/Kd) and controller parameters that remain stable across tasks.

What I did

  • Applied domain randomization during training to improve policy generalization before hardware tests.
  • Established safety procedures (reduced initial speeds, clear work area, incremental envelopes) for live trials.
  • Performed controller & joint tuning (Kp/Kd, filters) and iterative bring-up to reduce oscillations.
  • Carried out rapid corrective maintenance on end-effectors (dexterous hands), gaining insight into system interfaces and failure modes.

Results & learnings

  • Improved stability and responsiveness for simple motion patterns; safer live testing workflow.
  • Clear documentation of failure cases and a repeatable sim ‚Üí bench ‚Üí hardware loop for the team.
  • Deeper understanding of RL deployment limits and the value of incremental testing and quick iteration.

Tech

Isaac/Sim (for RL training visuals), Python/C++, ROS 2, unitree SDKs, control-gain tuning tools, rapid prototyping & repair for dexterous hands.
