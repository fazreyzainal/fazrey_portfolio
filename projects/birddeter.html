Automated Bird Deterrence Concept

Exploration of how a quadruped robot (Unitree Go2) could patrol and deter birds around restricted areas. I built a preliminary movement + patrol framework with Nav2, evaluated simple perception triggers for bird activity, and tested ArUco check-ins for localisation verification and proof-of-presence.

What is the project?

Design a patrol-route system that lets Go2 navigate between zones and react to detected bird activity without using invasive methods. I reused my Nav2/SLAM groundwork from the Go2 project to plan routes and experimented with fiducial markers (ArUco) as optional localisation anchors and attendance logging at key points.

Challenges

  • Outdoor robustness: variable lighting and clutter can reduce detector reliability.
  • Localisation drift: small errors accumulate on long patrols across open areas.
  • Non-invasive response: stay humane and safe while signalling deterrence.

Solutions

  • Patrol with Nav2: defined routes and waypoints; tuned costmaps for wider, outdoor-style spaces.
  • ArUco ‚Äúclock-in‚Äù points: robot verifies pose at markers; doubles as presence proof for audits.
  • Lightweight vision triggers: experimented with a simple bird detector/tracker to switch behaviours (announce, face target, shine safe indicator / laser pointer).

Results

  • A working patrol-and-respond loop on Go2 with optional ArUco verification.
  • Demonstrated feasibility and listed engineering considerations for real deployments (environmental robustness, safety, and autonomy boundaries).
