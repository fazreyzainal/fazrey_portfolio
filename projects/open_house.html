SIT Open House 2025 ‚Äì Interactive Humanoid Robot Booth Concept

Under tight event deadlines, I delivered a reliable public demo by prioritising core features, splitting work into clear subsystems, and running modular tests before final integration. My scope covered a drink-dispenser mechanism the humanoid could pick from, and Android UI tweaks so visitors could interact smoothly with the robot.

What is the project?

Design a booth experience where the humanoid consistently performs short interactions for crowds. For SIT Open House 2025, I proposed and implemented a drink-dispenser mechanism that presents a can in a uniform pick position for the robot. In parallel, I updated the existing Android application (built in Kotlin) to better fit the event theme and streamline visitor interaction.

The aim was not just a one-off demo, but a repeatable, event-ready flow with clear operator controls, safety, and quick recovery between runs.

Challenges

  • Subsystem integration: hardware (sensors and servos), microcontroller IO, robot pick pose, and booth comms.
  • Consistency for crowds: uniform can presentation, clean resets, and no jams during long sessions.
  • New stack quickly: learn Android Studio/Kotlin to modify the UI and logic for the event.

Solutions

  • Modular plan: split into dispenser mechanics, electronics & sensing, app/UI, and robot pick routine. Validate each module independently, then integrate.
  • Dispenser mechanism: rolling-based feed with a pickup bay; sensors and servos controlled by a microcontroller to present one can at a time.
  • 3D CAD & prints: iterated brackets and guides to remove snags and keep the can pose repeatable.
  • Android app tweaks (Kotlin): adjusted screens/prompts and event flow; ensured the operator had simple Start/Reset actions.
  • Operational checklists: booth bring-up, interface checks, motion tuning, and quick visual feedback calibration.

Results

  • Ran consistent live demos with clean resets and uniform can presentation for the humanoid pickup.
  • Reduced operator workload via a simplified UI and clearly documented procedures.
  • Learned to balance ambition vs. reliability so each subsystem performed under live conditions.

Tech

Mechanism design (3D CAD/printing), sensors & servos with microcontroller, Android Studio (Kotlin), ROS-side pick routine, booth comms & checklists.
