RoboSG! 2025 ‚Äì Interactive Tic-Tac-Toe Playing Humanoid

A public-friendly demo where a humanoid plays Tic-Tac-Toe against visitors. I focused on a robust state machine, quick operator controls, simple perception of the board, and clear guidance cues so the game stays smooth in a noisy exhibition hall.

What is the project?

Let any passerby play a fast game with the robot. The system walks the visitor through Start ‚Üí Player move ‚Üí Robot move ‚Üí Win/Draw ‚Üí Reset. Priority was on clarity, repeatability, and safe interaction so queues flow and the robot can run for hours.

Challenges

  • Event noise & pace: voice triggers unreliable; visitors rotate quickly; must reset instantly.
  • Perception simplicity: detect a 3√ó3 board and human marks reliably without heavy compute.
  • Game logic + motion: guarantee legal robot moves and stable arm trajectories near the board.
  • Operator workload: one person supervising needs obvious status & one-tap recovery.
  • Frequent shutdowns: inconsistent shutdowns occured while demoing to guests.

Solutions

  • Prompts & gestures: concise voice prompts cues to show ‚Äúyour turn / my turn‚Äù.
  • Move planner: legal-move set + simple win/defense strategy with pre-validated poses for each cell.
  • Operator panel: Start/Reset, skip turn, and health readouts (heartbeat, battery, network).
  • Rewiring of connectors: An unplugged loose wire cause circuit short which prompted the system safety protocol to shutdown, on-the-spot temporary fix to hold connnector in place.

Results

  • Consistent game loops with fast resets; queues kept moving and visitors understood the flow.
  • Stable arm motions and safe clearances around the board; no crowd-safety incidents.
  • Minimal operator intervention‚Äîone person could manage the booth comfortably.
  • Reduce sporadic shutdowns temporarily for the event.

Tech

ROS 2, Python/C++, pre-tuned arm poses, TTS/Audio prompts, simple web controller, Git/GitHub.
