Quadruped (Unitree Go2) Autonomous Navigation

End-to-end proof-of-concept for wireless indoor autonomy on Unitree Go2 using ROS 2 Nav2 + SLAM Toolbox, with safe teleop handover and obstacle avoidance. I evaluated multiple Unitree SDKs (official & community) and combined the strengths of the Unofficial ROS2 SDK (wireless + navigation) with CHAMP (navigation + obstacle avoidance in sim), then validated mapping, planning and connectivity across a real building floor.

What is the project?

The goal was to achieve reliable autonomous navigation on the Unitree Go2 with a fully wireless workflow. I compared Unitree‚Äôs official SDKs against the community Unofficial ROS2 SDK to understand navigation, obstacle avoidance, and connectivity trade-offs. I then used CHAMP to analyse navigation/obstacle-avoidance in simulation (with a simulated LiDAR and full ROS 2 graph), and finally deployed a merged approach: Unofficial ROS2 SDK for wireless comms + teleop + navigation, and a Nav2/SLAM pipeline tuned for indoor corridors and booth routes.

Challenges

  • SDK capability gaps: official SDKs lacked wireless connectivity and full navigation APIs; community SDK had wireless + navigation but no dynamic obstacle avoidance.
  • Sim-to-real consistency: matching CHAMP sim behaviour (sensing, transforms, ROS 2 graph) to real hardware.
  • Planner stability: oscillations and poor turning near tight corners; costmap inflation and layers needed careful tuning.
  • Reliable wireless in a multi-room environment; needed to quantify WebRTC round-trip latency at many points of interest.

How I overcame them

  • SDK decision via comparison: built a feature matrix (wireless, navigation, obstacle avoidance). Selected the Unofficial ROS2 SDK for deployment due to its wireless + navigation support; kept CHAMP to study obstacle-avoidance behaviour and map the ROS 2 graph.
  • Simulation bring-up: configured CHAMP with a simulated LiDAR; verified topics/TF using RQT graphs for both stationary and moving cases to mirror real-world nodes and transforms.
  • Mapping & planning: used SLAM Toolbox with loop-closure parameters and map clean-up; tuned local/global costmaps (inflation, voxel/obstacle layers) and DWB critics to remove corner oscillations.
  • Wireless tests (WebRTC): measured round-trip times across multiple points of interest and connection modes (e.g., STA-L/AP/STA-T), then chose the most stable configuration for the floor layout.

Results

  • Demonstrated wireless autonomous navigation on the Go2 with reliable localisation and dynamic obstacle avoidance along a full corridor loop.
  • Produced a floor map with annotated points of interest to reason about connectivity and planner behaviour.
  • Validated a merged architecture: Unofficial ROS2 SDK for wireless+navigation, Nav2/SLAM for autonomy, and CHAMP insights to guide obstacle-avoidance tuning.
  • Delivered a working prototype and an engineering write-up summarising SDK trade-offs, ROS 2 graph snapshots, and connectivity measurements.

Tech

ROS 2 (Nav2, SLAM Toolbox), C++/Python, OpenCV, MQTT/WebRTC, CHAMP, Linux/Jetson, Git/GitHub, RQT/TF tools.
